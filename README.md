# ***Siege***

---

## **What is Siege?**

Siege is a coup-inspired, action-filled, strategic card game where you have to lie and steal each other’s tokens to become the richest kingdom.

## **Setup**

- Shuffle the deck, and deal out 2 cards and 3 tokens to each player.
- Players take turns, starting with the player to the left of the dealer and proceeding counterclockwise.
- When the deck runs out, the dealer must reshuffle the used pile into the deck before anyone makes any moves.

## **On Your Turn…**

- Either place 1 card into the discard pile or draw a card from the deck.
  - Cards are placed face down, unless they is revealed.
- Declare what card it is (unless it is a double/triple).

## **Card Types**

- Siege has many cards that all drive the play loop of the game
- All of these cards can be split into 3 categories:
  - Attack
  - Defense
  - Wild
- Attack cards can be placed down on your turn.
  - All of them negatively affect others and/or positively affect you.
- Defense cards can be placed to block an attack card that is used on you.
- Wild cards can be used as attack or defense cards.

### **Attack**

- Assassin: Steals a token from a player of your choice
  - Identifier: As
  - \# of this card: 4x
- King: Steals a card of your choice from a player of your choice
  - Identifier: Kn
  - \# of this card: 4x
- Spy: Reveals a card of your choice from a player of your choice. The card stays revealed for the rest of the game. You can only use this card on other players with unrevealed cards.
  - Identifier: Sp
  - \# of this card: 4x
- Warden: Skips one turn of a player of your choice.
  - Identifier: Wd
  - \# of this card: 4x
- Inquisitor: The player you choose must reveal all of their cards, and you must then discard a card from your hand of your choice. If you do not have any other cards than the Inquisitor, you cannot claim it. You can only use this card on other players with unrevealed cards.
  - Identifier: Iq
  - \# of this card: 2x
- Fool: When used, give this card to another player. If this card is stolen, and only if it is **stolen**, from you, you must also give the thief a token, and the card is revealed. 
  - Identifier: Fl
  - \# of this card: 4x
- Merchant: Steal a token from a player of your choice, but in response, you must give them a card of your choice.
  - Identifier: Mc
  - \# of this card: 4x
- Herald: Upon use, reshuffle the used and deck piles into the deck.
  - Identifier: Hd
  - \# of this card: 4x

### **Defense**

- Guard: Use this card to block any action, even blocking. You can only block actions that negatively impact you.
  - Identifier: Gd
  - \# of this card: 4x
- Reflector: This card can only be used against a card that negatively impacts the user. Upon use, mirror the declared effect back to the attacker. The attacker’s effect is also cancelled on use.
  - Identifier: Rf
  - \# of this card: 4x

### **Wild**

- Jester: This card can be claimed as any card.
  - Identifier: Js
  - \# of this card: 2x
- Scourge: This card can be claimed as any card. If it is called out and the user isn’t lying, the target must give the user 2 tokens instead of 1\.
  - Identifier: Sc
  - \# of this card: 2x

## **Other Rules**

Other rules do not fit any of the previous sections, but still add importance to the game

### **Revealed Cards**

- When a card is revealed, you must turn it over in your hand so that the rank/identifier faces everyone else. 
  - When placing it, it must be placed face up on the discard pile.
- Even after the deck is reshuffled, the card must stay revealed.

### **Challenging and Lying**

- You can call anyone out if you think they are lying about what card/s they played.
- You must call them out after they play their card and before they complete the rest of their action.
- If you call them out, they must reveal their cards.
  - If they were lying, they must give you a token, and their action fails.
  - If they were telling the truth, you must give them 1 or more tokens, based on the cards that they played, and their action continues.
    - You must give them the tokens for the highest paying card they played.
- Even if you call them out, the user does not take back their cards.
- You cannot block challenge outcomes.

### **Doubles and Triples**

- On your turn, instead of playing one card, you can choose to play 2 or 3 of the same card, called doubles and triples, respectively.
- You can lie about doubles and triples, and challenging works the same for them.
- On playing a double, you must draw 3 new cards.
- On playing a triple,
  - If there are 2 players left, you must draw 5 new cards.
  - Otherwise, you must steal a token from all other players.
- You can only block a triple if it is a token steal, and blocking it only affects your token steal. (e.g., If you reflect a triple, everyone else still pays the attacker, but the attacker pays you a token.)

### **Sudden Siege**

- When only 2 players remain (unless the game started with 2 players), the game enters Sudden Siege.
- During Sudden Siege, all token steals are doubled, no exceptions.
- Player skips are also doubled during Sudden Siege.
- Sudden Siege lasts for the remainder of the game.

### **Hand Limit**

- You can only have a maximum of 12 cards in your hand.
- If you have to or want to draw more cards when you have 12, you must ignore the drawn cards
  - This means you can pass your turn if you have 12 cards in your hand.

## **Gamemodes**

Siege has 3 game modes of varying complexity:

- Classic Siege \- Gamemode for Siege beginners. Low complexity and teaches players the basics of Siege.
  - Has cards \[As, Kn, Sp, Wd, Gd, Js\]
- Normal/Original Siege \- Gamemode for intermediate Siege-ers. Adds more complex cards and the Fool (Fl) that is parasitic to promote urgency for players.
  - Has cards \[As, Kn, Sp, Wd, Iq, Fl, Gd, Rf, Js, Sc\]
- Super Siege \- Most complex Siege gamemode for pro players. Adds Merchant (Mc) and Herald (Hd).
  - Has cards \[As, Kn, Sp, Wd, Iq, Fl, Mc, Hd, Gd, Rf, Js, Sc\]
